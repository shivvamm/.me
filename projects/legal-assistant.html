01 / Problem

The context.

General-purpose LLMs hallucinate citations in legal domains — a dealbreaker when a mis-cited section could mislead a lawyer or a student. Indian legal texts are particularly dense with cross-references (IPC, CrPC, Evidence Act) that need precise retrieval, not vibes.

The goal: an assistant that answers IPC queries with grounded citations, navigates precedent, and admits uncertainty rather than confabulating.

02 / Solution

Specialized model + grounded retrieval.

01
Fine-tuned Gemma 2B

PEFT + LoRA adaptation on curated IPC + case-law corpus. 85% accuracy on complex query resolution.

02
Citation-Backed RAG

PDF ingestion, precedent retrieval, and structured citations — every answer points back to the source section and paragraph.

03
Multi-Agent Orchestration

Agents autonomously navigate legal documents and execute dynamic task critics for accuracy validation.
