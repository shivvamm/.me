01 / Problem

The context.

Insurance claims reviewers spend hours manually cross-checking services listed in policy docs against services billed in claim forms. A mismatch can mean fraud, overbilling, or compliance exposure — but the task is tedious and error-prone at volume.

Goal: an autonomous agent that reads both documents, reasons across line items, flags mismatches with confidence, and routes edge cases to human reviewers.

02 / Solution

Reasoning pipeline.

01
Structured Extraction

Prompt pipelines + schema validation to pull policy.services[] and claim.line_items[] from mixed-format PDFs.

02
Cross-Verification

Per-item reasoning chain that matches, flags, or escalates — with confidence scoring on every decision.

03
Reviewer Handoff

Structured report with mismatch evidence, ready for a human reviewer to rubber-stamp or override.

03 / My Contributions

What I built.

A.
Multi-step reasoning pipeline

Chained prompts + tool calls to extract, compare, and explain mismatches. 91% precision in production.

B.
Prompt engineering + evaluation

Iteratively tuned prompts with regression tests against golden-set claim-policy pairs — drove precision up without sacrificing recall.

C.
Production integration

Shipped into the claims-review workflow with reviewer UI, feedback loops, and per-decision audit trail.
